Instructions

Drawing
Curves should have sufficient margin
The curves should have sufficient margin between them, overlapping or close lines are hard to interpret
Drawing with dark pen
To recognise your level curves, we prefer a dark and thick marker. Using a lighter pen will likely not work
Close your curves
To avoid false readings, close your curves, dont leave any open-ended lines
Scanning
Four corners visible
To scan the level curves, we need to have the four corners visible
Drawing should not be too far
Scanning works best from a distance of 0.5 meters from the piece of paper. For best the best result try to scan the paper as close to parallel as possible. This is easiest if you put your drawing on a table instead of hnewing it.
Proper lighting
Level curves are hard to distinguish from the background in a low light setting. Try to use natural daylight or a propper ceiling light for the best results. Be careful of sharp or dark shadows on the drawing, as they might get interpreted as part of your drawing.
